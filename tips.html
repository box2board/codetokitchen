Kitchen Tips

Practical cooking tips to make your time in the kitchen easier and more efficient.

  • Salt early and often

    Season foods in stages to build layers of flavor.

  • Bring proteins to room temp

    Let meat sit 15–20 min before cooking for even doneness.

  • Pan sauce 101

    Deglaze with stock and finish with butter for a quick sauce.

  • Sheet-pan spacing = crisping

    Leave space between items so they roast, not steam.

  • Thermometer > guesswork

    Use an instant-read thermometer for perfect doneness.

  • Batch aromatics

    Prep garlic, onions, and herbs in bulk for quick meals.

  • Starch water saves pasta sauces

    Reserve pasta water to thicken and emulsify sauces.

  • Rest meats before slicing

    Allow juices to redistribute for moist results.

  • Freeze herbs in oil

    Preserve fresh herbs in ice cube trays with oil.

  • Taste in stages

    Taste as you cook to adjust seasoning on the fly.

  • Sharpen knives regularly

    A sharp knife is safer and more efficient.

  • Mise en place = speed

    Organize ingredients beforehand for smoother cooking.
